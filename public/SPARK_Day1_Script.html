Production Notes

Voiceover
Single narrator. Calm, measured, authoritative. Not hype. Not urgency. Certainty. Think Morgan Freeman meets James Baldwin — but contemporary, present, not nostalgic.
Music
Minimal. Single kora note or mbira phrase at the open. Under the voiceover: a low, modern pulse — sub-bass + ambient texture. No drums until Scene 5. Then: a single hit on "remembered."
Color grade
Dark and warm. Deep shadows. Gold highlights. No bright whites. The aesthetic should feel like a museum at night — reverent, precise, alive with meaning.
Pacing
Slower than TikTok convention. Every pause is intentional. Let the parallel structures breathe. The viewer should feel the weight of recognition, not the speed of content.
On-screen text
Space Mono. Gold on black. Sentence case. No transitions — clean cuts only. Text appears word by word at key moments, never all at once.
The student
Scene 4 and 5 require a real student. Not an actor. Someone who has actually completed the lab — or is about to. Their presence is the proof. Authenticity is the asset.
Ifa visuals
Source abstract representations — not ritual-specific imagery. The point is the mathematical architecture, not the ceremony. Geometric diagrams of the 256 states work well.
Sora prompts
Scene 1: "Extreme close-up, slow motion, dark warm tones, brass palm nuts placed on woven mat by an unseen hand, cinematic, shallow depth of field." Scene 4: "Young Black teenager looking directly into camera, neutral confident expression, abstract gold geometric patterns behind them, dark studio, 4K."
