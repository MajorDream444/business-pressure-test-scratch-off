Hanzo Prompt Stack — Day 5
Prompt 1 · NIL Contract Literacy Guide
+
Teach me the essential clauses and red flags in NIL contracts. Format this as an actionable checklist I can use every time I review a deal. Cover these areas: 1. Exclusivity clauses — what's acceptable vs. dangerous 2. Usage rights — who owns the content I create? 3. Compensation structure — lump sum vs. performance-based vs. equity 4. Term and renewal — how long and who controls extension? 5. Termination clauses — how do I get out if things go wrong? 6. Likeness rights — how far can they use my name/image? 7. FTC compliance — what disclosure do I need to make? For each area: explain in plain English, give 1 red flag example, and give 1 acceptable version of that clause. Note: This is educational only — always have a licensed attorney review actual contracts.
Prompt 2 · Counter-Offer Scripts
+
Write 3 professional counter-offer scripts for common NIL negotiation scenarios. My info: [ATHLETE NAME, SPORT, FOLLOWER LEVEL] Scenario 1 — They offered below my rate: They offered $[X]. My rate for this deliverable is $[Y]. Write a counter that: thanks them, acknowledges the offer, presents my rate confidently, and offers a bridge (maybe a smaller scope at their budget). Scenario 2 — They want full exclusivity in my category: They want me to not work with any other [CATEGORY] brands for [TIME]. Write a counter that: values the relationship, explains why full exclusivity at that rate doesn't work, proposes category exclusivity for a premium instead. Scenario 3 — They want to own the content I create forever: Write a counter that: agrees to 12-month licensed usage with renewal option, retains my rights to the content after the term, and proposes a reasonable fee for extended usage. Each script: professional, confident, not apologetic. Under 100 words each.
