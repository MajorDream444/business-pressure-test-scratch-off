Hanzo Prompt Stack — Day 1
Prompt 1 · Niche Extractor
+
You are a brand strategist helping a creator define their niche. Ask me 5 rapid-fire questions about my background, skills, and the problems I've solved for others. After I answer, synthesize my responses into a niche statement in this format: "I help [specific audience] achieve [specific outcome] through [unique method/lens]." Then give me 3 alternative versions. Keep each under 20 words.
Prompt 2 · Pillar Builder
+
Based on my niche statement: [PASTE YOUR NICHE HERE] Generate 5 potential content pillars for my brand. Each pillar should: - Be a topic I can create 50+ pieces on - Directly serve my target audience - Be distinct from the other pillars - Connect to a monetization angle Format: Pillar Name · One sentence description · Example content angle
Prompt 3 · Voice Encoder
+
I'm going to paste 3 examples of my writing below. Analyze my voice and give me: 1. 5 words that describe my tone 2. 3 things I should NEVER sound like 3. A "voice rulebook" — 5 rules for how I write I'll use this to train AI to write in my voice. [PASTE 3 WRITING SAMPLES]

The before/after 9. Controversial opinion 10. Vivid scene-setting Make each hook under 2 sentences. Write in my voice: [PASTE YOUR VOICE RULES FROM DAY 1]
Prompt 2 · Long-Form Drafter
+
Write a long-form content piece on this topic: [ANGLE] Hook to use: [YOUR CHOSEN HOOK] Audience: [AUDIENCE] Voice rules: [PASTE YOUR VOICE RULES] Word count: 900–1100 words Structure: - Hook (2–3 sentences) - The problem (why this matters) - The insight (your unique perspective) - The framework or steps (the meat) - The story or proof (makes it real) - The call to reflection or action Do NOT make it sound like a blog post template. Make it sound like I'm talking to one specific person.

Hanzo Prompt Stack — Day 4
Prompt 1 · Platform Repurposer
+
Here is my source content piece: [PASTE DAY 3 PIECE] Repurpose this into a LinkedIn post using these rules: - 300–400 words - Hook in line 1 that stops the scroll - Short sentences and white space (LinkedIn formatting) - Personal tone, first-person - End with a question to drive comments - Subtle CTA in last line - Voice: [PASTE VOICE RULES] Do not summarize — reimagine it for LinkedIn's audience and format.
Prompt 2 · Video Script Converter
+
Convert this piece into a 75-second video script: [PASTE SOURCE CONTENT] Format: HOOK (0–5 sec): One sentence that creates immediate curiosity or controversy SETUP (5–15 sec): The problem or context in 1–2 sentences POINTS (15–60 sec): 3 punchy points, 10–15 sec each CTA (60–75 sec): One clear action for the viewer Write in spoken language — contractions, short sentences, natural pauses. Add [PAUSE] and [EMPHASIS] where relevant. Voice: [VOICE RULES]
Prompt 3 · Thread + Email Splitter
+
From this source content: [PASTE] Task A — Twitter/X Thread: Write a 10-tweet thread. Tweet 1 = hook. Tweets 2–9 = one insight each (under 240 chars). Tweet 10 = CTA + what's coming next. Number each tweet. Task B — Email Newsletter: Write a 200-word email version. Subject line options: give me 3. Opening: a personal observation, not a summary. Body: one key insight expanded. Close: conversational sign-off + CTA. Voice for both: [VOICE RULES]

Hanzo Prompt Stack — Day 5
Prompt 1 · Offer Bridge Builder
+
I create content about: [CONTENT TOPIC / PILLAR] My offer is: [YOUR OFFER — product, service, program, consultation] Write an Offer Bridge statement — a 1–2 sentence connection between my content and my offer that feels NATURAL, not salesy. The reader should feel like the offer is the obvious next step after reading my content. Then write 5 CTA variations in these styles: 1. Direct ("Work with me here: [link]") 2. Soft ("If this resonated, there's more at: [link]") 3. Curiosity ("What I didn't mention here is in: [link]") 4. Proof-based ("The people who hired me got X — details: [link]") 5. Invitation ("I'm taking 5 people through this live — apply: [link]")
Prompt 2 · CTA Embedder
+
Here is my content piece: [PASTE PIECE] Here is my offer: [OFFER] Here is my preferred CTA style for this piece: [DIRECT / SOFT / CURIOSITY / PROOF / INVITATION] Rewrite the closing 2–3 paragraphs to naturally lead into this CTA. The offer should feel like it emerged from the content — not like an ad appended to the end. The transition should be invisible. Keep my voice: [VOICE RULES]
